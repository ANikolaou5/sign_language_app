Y

American Sign Language

Key Characteristics

  • check_circle Extended Outer Fingers: The thumb and the pinky finger are fully extended outward.
  • check_circle Tucked Center: The index, middle, and ring fingers remain folded flat against the palm.

Common Clarifications

Don't confuse 'Y' with these similar signs:

  • I Letter I

    The pinky is up, but the thumb is tucked across the fingers. For 'Y', the thumb must be extended.

  • J Letter J

    Uses the 'I' handshape (pinky only) but adds a swooping motion. 'Y' is a static handshape.

  • L Letter L

    The thumb is out, but the index finger is up. For 'Y', it is the pinky finger that is up.
